Hey, I'm Adrian.

I do some things.

I'm a freelance front-end web designer/developer, stationed in (read: near) Detroit, Michigan, looking to make this ol' World Wide Web my canvas. I believe in hand-crafted code, responsive design, accessibility, and intuitive user-interfaces. I write my HTML, CSS, Javascript, and jQuery code from the bottom up, but can find my way home in a CMS or framework setting as well.

I'm also a graduate student of Information Management & User-Experience at Wayne State University's School of Library of Information Science, and my day job is as a reference intern at a gorgeous public library. I'm a strong supporter of information literacy and transparency, cultural preservation, and libraries for the "future." Metadata is fun, digital curation is fun, helping people is very fun.

I'm very into words. Writing has been a mainstay in my life since I first picked up a rock and etched my feelings into it. I'm educated as a historian, and have experience writing almost every type of style there is. There are a lot of words in my head, and I'm loving every chance I get to use them. If editing is your thing, I've been around MLA, APA, and Chicago style guides for a while.

Do you like photography? I do too. I've been taking pictures since 2007, and even freelanced for a bit during my undergrad. I have experience with event, portrait, and commercial photography, and I find the sound of the shutter very relaxing. Adobe products have been my friend for a few now, and I'm very proficient with Adobe Bridge, Photoshop CS6, and Illustrator CS6.

You may find me playing hockey or disc golf, reading or playing Magic: The Gathering. I love history, philosophy, and space. I also love my two monster pugs and my little rescued cat, my Starbucks Gold Card, The Inbetweeners, 70s soft rock, and trying to be the happiest person I can possibly be.
